Biography of MS Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, is one of India's most iconic and celebrated cricketers, renowned for his exceptional leadership, calm demeanor under pressure, and incredible success across all formats of the game.

Early Life and Background:

  • Birth: MS Dhoni was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand), India.
  • Education: He attended DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir, Shyamali, Ranchi, where he excelled in various sports, including cricket, football, and badminton.

Cricket Journey:

  • Early Career: Dhoni began his cricketing journey as a talented wicketkeeper-batsman in domestic cricket for Bihar (later Jharkhand).
  • Breakthrough: His big break came in 2004 when he captained the India A team on a tour of Zimbabwe and Kenya.
  • International Debut: Dhoni made his ODI debut for India against Bangladesh in December 2004 and quickly rose through the ranks due to his aggressive batting style and sharp wicketkeeping skills.

Major Achievements:

  • Captaincy: Dhoni became the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats in 2007, leading India to numerous victories, including the ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.
  • Influence: Known as 'Captain Cool,' Dhoni earned a reputation for his composed leadership, tactical acumen, and ability to make critical decisions under pressure.
  • Batting Records: He holds several records in international cricket, including the most international matches as captain for India, the most not-outs in ODIs, and the most stumpings by any wicketkeeper in international cricket.

Personal Life:

  • Family: Dhoni married Sakshi Singh Rawat in 2010, and they have a daughter named Ziva.
  • Beyond Cricket: Apart from cricket, Dhoni has been involved in various business ventures, including owning the Chennai Super Kings franchise in the Indian Premier League (IPL) and being an honorary Lieutenant Colonel in the Indian Army.

Legacy:

  • Iconic Status: Dhoni's influence extends beyond cricket, making him one of India's most admired and respected sports personalities.
  • Retirement: After a successful international career spanning over 16 years, Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket in August 2020, marking the end of an illustrious era in Indian cricket.

MS Dhoni's journey from a small-town boy to a cricketing legend has inspired millions around the world, leaving an indelible mark on the sport and securing his place among the all-time greats of cricket.

Records and Achievements of MS Dhoni

Records

Tests:

  • Most runs by an Indian wicket-keeper (4876)
  • Most number of sixes by an Indian captain (78)
  • Most dismissals by an Indian and fifth most by any wicket-keeper (294)

ODIs:

  • Most wins by an Indian captain and second most overall (110)
  • Second most runs as captain (6641)
  • Third most number of matches as captain (200)
  • First player to pass 10,000 runs with an average of over 50
  • Most not-outs (84)
  • Highest score by a wicket-keeper (183*)
  • Highest eighth wicket partnership for India (100* with Bhuvneshwar Kumar)
  • Most dismissals in an innings (6) and career (432) by an Indian wicket-keeper
  • Most stumpings by any wicket-keeper (123)

T20Is:

  • Second most matches as captain (72)
  • Most T20I innings (76) and runs (1,153) before scoring a fifty
  • Most stumpings as wicket-keeper (34)
  • Most catches as wicket keeper in a T20I innings (5)

Combined:

  • Most international matches as captain (332)
  • Most stumpings (195) and only wicket-keeper to make 150 stumpings
  • Third most dismissals as a wicket-keeper (829)
  • Sixth most sixes in career (359)

Honors

India

  • T20 World Cup: 2007
  • Asia Cup: 2010, 2016
  • Cricket World Cup: 2011
  • ICC Champions Trophy: 2013

Chennai Super Kings

  • Indian Premier League: 2010, 2011, 2018, 2021, 2023
  • Champions League: 2010, 2014

Individual

  • MTV Youth Icon of the Year: 2006
  • Honorary doctorate degree by De Montfort University: 2011
  • CNN-News18 Indian of the Year: 2011
  • Major Dhyanchand Khel Ratna award: 2008
  • Padma Shri: 2009
  • Honorary Lieutenant Colonel, Parachute Regiment of the Indian Territorial Army: 2011
  • Castrol Indian Cricketer of the Year: 2011
  • ICC People's Choice Award: 2013
  • Padma Bhushan: 2018
  • ICC ODI Player of the Year: 2008, 2009
  • ICC Men's ODI Team of the Year: 2006, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014 (captain in 2009, 2011–2014)
  • ICC Men's ODI team of the decade: 2011–2020 (captain and wicket-keeper)
  • ICC Men's T20I team of the decade: 2011–2020 (captain and wicket-keeper)
  • ICC Spirit of the cricket award of the decade: 2011–2020

In 2019, Jharkhand Cricket Association named the JSCA stadium's south stand after Dhoni. In 2023, the Mumbai Cricket Association (MCA) decided to honor him by dedicating seats (J282–J286) at the Wankhede Stadium where he hit the winning shot in 2011 World Cup final.
